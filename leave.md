Leave Flow and Management Summary
1.	Leave Types and Entitlements
Leave Type	Entitlement	Eligibility	Key Conditions
Annual Leave	U2 and above: 36 days
U7–U3: 30 days
U8: 24 days 	Full-time public officers	Vests on 1st January annually; must be taken within the calendar year 
Sick Leave	90 days on full pay (extendable to 180 days)	All officers with medical recommendation	Requires Government Medical Officer's report for absences >2 working days 
Maternity Leave	60 working days on full pay 	All female officers, regardless of status	Can start between 36–38 weeks; additional days offset against earned leave

Leave Without Pay	Up to 5 years (renewable once)	Officers joining international organisations or projects	Not leave-earning; increments deferred; job not protected except for international quotas 

2.	Leave Approval Stages
The approval hierarchy follows the chain of command in the Public Service:
Stage	Role	Responsibility
Stage 1	Employee	Submits leave application form (or electronic system) at least 2 weeks in advance 
Stage 2	Head of Unit / Supervisor	Reviews, recommends, and forwards to Responsible Officer 
Stage 3	Responsible Officer	Approves leave as scheduled, approves part, or defers to a later date 
Stage 4	HR / Records	Updates leave records, maintains leave roster, and ensures compliance with carry-over rules
Special Approvals:
•	Permanent Secretaries: For all staff in their MDA.
•	Deputy Governor: For Permanent Secretaries and senior officers .
•	Service Commission: For long-term study leave .

3.	Leave Tracking and Management
3.1 Leave Rosters
•	Leave rosters for the ensuing year must be available by 31st December of the preceding year .
•	Copy exhibited on Notice Boards for staff visibility.
•	Heads of Unit must ensure staff take leave when due .
3.2 Carry-Over and Forfeiture
Rule	Description
General Rule	Annual leave cannot be accumulated from one calendar year to another. Failure to take leave within the calendar year results in automatic forfeiture .
Exception	Carry-over is only permitted if the officer submitted a written request for approval by 15th December of the preceding year .
Accumulated Leave Issue	A public officer who has accumulated leave (e.g., 140 days over 4 years) cannot be forced to take it without having applied for carry-over approval .
3.3 Tracking Mechanism
•	Manual: Paper-based leave application forms processed through supervisors and HR.
•	Electronic: The PMS will digitize the process, enabling real-time tracking of leave balances, approvals, and history.
•	Integration: Attendance data is linked to performance appraisal scores, ensuring alignment between duty, leave, and performance management.
